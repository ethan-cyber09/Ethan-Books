The Princess & The Pea

Z z Z

Once upon a time, in a kingdom full of grand castles and rolling green hills, there lived a Prince named Arthur. Arthur had everything a prince could want, except for one thing: a princess to share his life with. But Arthur was very particular. He didn't just want any princess; he wanted a *real* princess.

So, Arthur packed his royal suitcases and traveled all over the world. He met princesses in grand palaces, princesses in icy castles, and princesses in desert tents. But every time, something was just a little bit off.

🌧️🏰⛈️

One gloomy November evening, a terrible storm rolled into the kingdom. The wind howled like a hungry wolf, thunder crashed so loudly the castle walls shook, and rain poured down in heavy, freezing sheets. Just as the royal family was sitting down to dinner, there was a loud *BANG, BANG, BANG* at the heavy oak doors.

The old King went to open it. Standing there in the freezing rain was a young girl. But good heavens, what a spectacular mess she was! Her hair was plastered to her face, her beautiful dress was covered in mud, and water squelched out of the toes of her shoes. She looked like a drowned rat!

"Good evening, Your Majesty," the girl said politely, shivering in the cold. "My carriage broke down in the mud. I am a real princess, and I humbly ask for shelter from this awful storm."

"A real princess? Looking like that?" the old King thought to himself.

👸🥶👑

But the clever old Queen had an idea. "We'll soon find out if she is who she says she is," she whispered to herself. She smiled warmly at the dripping girl. "Come in, come in! We will prepare the royal guest room for you at once."

The Queen marched upstairs to the grandest bedroom in the castle. She ordered the servants to take all the blankets, sheets, and mattresses off the giant bed. Then, she reached into her pocket, pulled out a single, tiny, hard, green garden pea, and placed it right in the middle of the wooden bedframe.

Next, she ordered her servants to bring twenty of the thickest, heaviest mattresses in the castle and stack them on top of the pea. "More!" the Queen commanded. "Bring twenty of the fluffiest eiderdown feather beds and put them on top of the mattresses!"

🛏️🪜🥱

When they were finished, the bed was so incredibly high that the poor princess had to use a tall wooden ladder just to climb into it!

The next morning, the sun was shining, and the royal family gathered for breakfast. The princess walked in, looking absolutely exhausted. She had dark circles under her eyes and was walking with a stiff limp.

"Good morning, my dear," the Queen said with a sly smile. "How did you sleep?"

🤕🥱👎

"Oh, please forgive me, Your Majesties, but I slept terribly!" the princess groaned, rubbing her sore back. "I tossed and turned all night long! Heaven only knows what was hiding in that bed, but I was lying on something so incredibly hard that I am black and blue all over my body. It was a nightmare!"

Prince Arthur dropped his spoon. He beamed with joy! Now they knew for absolute certain that she was a real princess. She had felt the tiny, little green pea right through the twenty thick mattresses and the twenty fluffy feather beds. Nobody but a true, delicate, and sensitive princess could possibly feel such a thing.

🌟 The Moral of the Story 🌟

True qualities cannot be hidden, no matter how much you try to cover them up!
